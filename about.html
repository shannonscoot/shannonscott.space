Shannon Scott is a designer working across space, image, and form, guided by a deep connection to nature, ecology, and community. The work moves fluidly between architecture, visual art, and conceptual design, using each as a way to explore how people experience the world around them. Projects are approached as living systems—where light, material, movement, and texture shape feeling as much as function.

At its heart, this work is about connection: between people and place, between the built and the natural, and between imagination and lived experience. Design becomes a quiet form of storytelling—one that invites curiosity, participation, and a deeper sense of belonging.
